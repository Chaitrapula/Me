Everyone tells you the Taj Mahal can't live up to the hype. Everyone is wrong. You walk through the gateway, the marble comes into view, and the whole crowd goes quiet at the same moment. It does that to people.

## Go before the sun

The trick is to get there for the opening, before the heat and the tour buses. The marble is built to play with light. At dawn it runs soft and pink, by mid-morning it's blinding white, and the carving you can't see from a distance turns out to be inlaid flowers in coloured stone, all done by hand four hundred years ago.

Shah Jahan built it for Mumtaz Mahal, and somehow a monument to grief ended up being one of the most peaceful places I've stood in.

## A small, smug moment

I got my silhouette shot with the dome behind me and felt very pleased with myself. So did about three hundred other people. Worth it anyway.

> Stand still for a minute before you reach for the camera. The photo will still be there.
