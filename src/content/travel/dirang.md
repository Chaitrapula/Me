Everyone races through Dirang on the way to Tawang. They shouldn't. This is the part of Arunachal where the mountains ease off for a while — a wide green valley sitting at a gentler altitude, with a river running through the middle of it and orchards climbing the slopes on both sides.

## Blue hour in the valley

We rolled in at dusk, the cloud sitting low on the forested ridge and the village lights just coming on across the fields. The river ran fast and brown below the road, swollen with monsoon. It's the kind of view that makes you go quiet and stop fiddling with your phone.

## Water everywhere

The monsoon turns this whole stretch loud. A waterfall I'd have called modest in the dry season was thundering off the cliff, throwing spray halfway across the meadow. I got close enough to feel it on my face and not much closer.

## The valley's smaller residents

Sangti Valley, just outside town, is grazing country. Somewhere between the orchards and the river I met a baby goat, and that was more or less the end of my plans for the morning. Some travel highlights have four legs and no idea they're a highlight.

- Stop here for a night instead of blowing through to Tawang.
- The valley is best early, before the cloud lifts.
- Say hi to the goats. They run the place.

> Tawang is the destination. Dirang is the reminder to slow down and enjoy the road.
