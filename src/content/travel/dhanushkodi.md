Dhanushkodi sits at the very tip of Rameswaram island, a thin finger of sand pointing toward Sri Lanka. The sea runs along both sides of the road, calm and impossibly blue, until the land simply stops.

## The end of the road

A cyclone wiped the town off the map in 1964, and it was never rebuilt. What's left are bleached ruins, a few fishing families, and a quiet that feels older than the ruins themselves. The water is the kind of turquoise you don't believe until you're standing in it.

## Whose town it is now

The birds have taken over. Terns and gulls work the shallows in long lines, lifting all at once when a boat drifts too close. I could have watched them for hours, and more or less did.

> Go at low light, early or late. The heat is real, and so is the magic.
