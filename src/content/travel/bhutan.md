Bhutan is the only country I've stepped into outside India, and it set the bar high. You reach the eastern side overland, rolling out of the Assam plains and up to a painted gateway that says, plainly, "Thank you for visiting India." A few steps later you're in Druk Yul — the Land of the Thunder Dragon.

## Into the fog

The mountains here keep their own weather. We climbed into cloud so thick the road disappeared a few metres ahead, prayer flags materialising out of the white and then dissolving back into it. I found a quiet corner to just sit and watch the mist move. Some of my favourite minutes of the whole trip happened in that grey, soft silence.

## The markets

Down in the towns, life gathers around the markets. Women sell greens and ginger laid out on stone under an old banyan, everyone unhurried, everyone seeming to know everyone. I poked around the vegetable stalls, more for the company than the produce.

## Small things I loved

- The apple trees, heavy with deep-red fruit you could reach up and pick.
- Prayer flags strung across every ridge, pass, and rooftop.
- How completely the fog changes a place, and how comfortable everyone is inside it.

> India raised me; Bhutan reminded me how big and quiet the world can feel just across a line on the map.
