I spend my workdays building things in the cloud. On the best weekends I get to climb into the real ones. Meeshapulimala is where I go to do exactly that.

## The climb

At a little over 2,600 metres, Meeshapulimala is the second-highest peak in South India. The trek starts in the tea country above Munnar and works up through shola forest into open rolling grassland that stretches as far as you can see. There's a rhythm to it: climb, breathe, turn around, lose your breath again at the view.

## Into the white

Near the top you cross the cloud line. One minute the whole valley is laid out below you, the next you're inside a moving wall of white and the world shrinks to the few metres of trail in front of you. Then the wind shifts and it all opens up again. It never stopped feeling like magic.

- Start before dawn so you summit before the clouds close in for good.
- Good shoes beat everything else in your bag.
- Carry water and snacks. The grassland has views, not shops.

> The cloud on my laptop pays the bills. This is the one I climb for.
