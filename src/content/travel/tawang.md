Tawang hides in the far corner of Arunachal Pradesh, near the seam where India meets Bhutan and Tibet. You earn it slowly. The road climbs over Sela Pass at almost 4,000 metres, and by the time the valley opens up the air is so thin that every step up a staircase makes you stop and breathe.

## The monastery on the hill

The town folds around its monastery, one of the largest in the country and centuries old. Strings of prayer flags run from every rooftop and bridge, and they never stop moving. You hear them before you see them.

I spent a morning on the monastery steps watching the place wake up. A young monk, maybe eight years old, decided I was the most interesting thing there and spent twenty minutes trying to make me laugh. He won.

## What I'd tell you before you go

- Give yourself a day to acclimatise before you do anything strenuous.
- Carry layers. The sun is fierce and the shade is freezing, often within the same minute.
- The drive is the trip. Don't rush it.

> Some places you visit. Tawang you arrive at, slowly, and it changes the pace of everything after.
